# PlayDays styleguide

## Design direction
Warm, calm, modern, and trustworthy. The app should feel like a capable parent-sidekick, not a toy and not a sterile SaaS dashboard.

## Visual principles
- Mobile-first, thumb-friendly, generous tap targets
- Soft surfaces and rounded cards are fine, but hierarchy must stay strong
- Use warmth and clarity over trendiness
- Keep copy plainspoken and specific
- Prefer one strong CTA per section

## Interaction principles
- Fast empty-state guidance matters as much as “loaded” states
- Demo-mode should be explicit and helpful, not confusing
- Loading states should explain what is being prepared
- Returning-user flows should feel calmer and faster than first-run flows

## Component expectations
- Buttons, inputs, selects, sheets, cards, badges should come from existing shadcn-style primitives
- Hover/focus/disabled/loading states must be visible
- Mobile nav should remain understandable in one glance
- Cards should avoid feeling samey; section purpose should be visually distinct

## Copy tone
- Specific, practical, parent-aware
- Never fluffy or consultant-y
- Default to realistic promises instead of magic claims

## Anti-patterns
- Generic “AI app” visual tropes
- Vague CTA copy like `Get started` when a more concrete label fits
- Over-relying on decorative gradient treatment without stronger hierarchy
- Long explanatory paragraphs before action is possible
