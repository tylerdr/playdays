# PlayDays ICPs

## Primary ICP: overwhelmed parent of 1-2 young kids
- Usually has toddlers/preschoolers (roughly ages 1-6)
- Often juggling naps, snacks, weather, sibling mismatch, and depleted attention
- Wants a fast answer to “what should we do today?”
- Values realistic, low-friction ideas over aspirational Pinterest-parent content
- Likely using phone one-handed in short bursts

### Core pains
- Decision fatigue every morning
- Too many tabs/bookmarks/social posts, not enough actionable plans
- Outings fail because weather/nap windows/kid energy are ignored
- Generic activity lists are not tuned to their actual children
- Parent guilt when the day feels chaotic or under-stimulating

### Desired outcomes
- Get one practical plan fast
- Feel less mentally loaded
- Have flexible backup options when the day shifts
- Discover nearby kid-friendly places without separate research
- Build confidence that the app “gets” their family rhythm

## Secondary ICP: stay-at-home or flexible-schedule parent seeking structure
- Uses the app more habitually for daily rhythm
- Wants both at-home activities and outing anchors
- Cares about repeatability, saved ideas, and day pacing

## Tertiary ICP: co-parent / caregiver / grandparent helping for the day
- Needs context quickly
- Benefits from clear timeline blocks, low-prep options, and saved places
- Less likely to customize deeply, more likely to need a fast default

## Messaging notes
### What should resonate
- “One good plan for today”
- “Built for real parent constraints”
- “Weather-smart, nap-aware, low-friction”
- “Better than opening 12 tabs or doom-scrolling for ideas”

### What to avoid
- sounding like a generic AI productivity assistant
- overselling “magic personalization” before persistent learning/auth is truly shipped
- positioning as an education app, marketplace, or broad parenting community unless those products are actually built
